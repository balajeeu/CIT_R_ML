This dataset is originally from the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases. The objective is to predict based on diagnostic measurements whether a patient has diabetes.

Additional details about the attributes

  • Pregnancies: Number of times pregnant.
  • Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration a 2 hours in an oral glucose tolerance test.
  • BloodPressure: Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg).
  • SkinThickness: Triceps skin fold thickness (mm).
  • Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml).
  • BMI: Body mass index (weight in kg/(height in m)^2).
  • DiabetesPedigreeFunction: Diabetes pedigree function.
  • Age: Age (years).
  • Outcome: Class variable (0 or 1).
    • 0 - Patient did not have Diabetes
    • 1 - Patient have Diabetes

4) Data Visualisation and validations

ggplot(melt(diabetes),aes(value)) + geom_histogram(fill = "#4C72B0") + facet_wrap(~variable,scales = "free_x")

Anomalous Values are imputed

#Blood Pressure
diabetes$BloodPressure[diabetes$BloodPressure<55] = 55 
#Glucose
err_glucose = quantile(diabetes$Glucose,p = .01)
diabetes$Glucose[diabetes$Glucose<err_glucose] = mean(diabetes$Glucose)
#SkinThickness
diabetes$SkinThickness[diabetes$SkinThickness==0] = mean(diabetes$SkinThickness)
#Insulin
diabetes$Insulin[diabetes$Insulin==0] = mean(diabetes$Insulin)
#BMI
diabetes$BMI[diabetes$BMI==0] = mean(diabetes$BMI)

7) Random Forest

                        data = train,

                        mtry = 5,
                        replace = T,
                        importance = T)
